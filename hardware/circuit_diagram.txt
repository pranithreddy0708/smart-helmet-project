=======================================================
RASPBERRY PI 4 TO ESP32 SERIAL CONNECTION
=======================================================

  Raspberry Pi 4               ESP32 DevKit
  GPIO 14 (TX) ────────────── GPIO 3 (RX)
  GPIO 15 (RX) ────────────── GPIO 1 (TX)
  GND          ────────────── GND

  ⚠ NOTE: RPi GPIO is 3.3V. ESP32 RX is 3.3V tolerant.
    No level shifter needed for this direction.
    If using 5V ESP32 modules, add voltage divider on TX→RX.

=======================================================
POWER SUPPLY OPTIONS
=======================================================

OPTION A — Power Bank (prototyping):
  Power Bank (5V 3A USB-C) ──── Raspberry Pi 4 (USB-C)
  Raspberry Pi 4 (USB-A) ─────── ESP32 (USB)

OPTION B — Motorcycle Battery (permanent):
  Bike 12V Battery (+) ──── Buck Converter IN(+)
  Bike 12V GND ──────────── Buck Converter IN(-)
  Buck Converter OUT(+) ─── RPi 5V (GPIO pin 2)
  Buck Converter OUT(-) ─── RPi GND (GPIO pin 6)

  Use: XL4016 or LM2596 Buck Converter (12V → 5V/3A)

=======================================================
LCD I2C 16×2 PINOUT
=======================================================

  LCD I2C Backpack        ESP32
  ─────────────────────────────
  Pin 1: GND ──────────── GND
  Pin 2: VCC ──────────── 3.3V
  Pin 3: SDA ──────────── GPIO 21
  Pin 4: SCL ──────────── GPIO 22

  I2C Address: Usually 0x27 (check with I2C scanner)
  Library: LiquidCrystal_I2C
